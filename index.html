Scrollytelling templates

For Multimedia Storytelling

Marion Walton, University of Cape Town

Overview

This is a basic intro to multimedia scrollytelling that demonstrates scrollytelling techniques for use by Javascript novices. Templates adapted from Bill Shander's LinkedIn Tutorial and two of Russel Goldenberg's templates. Examples all use media from environmental activist Mina Guli's #runblue campaign.

Start by planning your story. Choose the visuals first and then write to picture. If you don't have final media available yet, use placeholders and sketches for your planning but always review the text once you have the media available. Keep your text simple and short.

Decide what the media (image or video) is doing in the story.

  • Informational (foreground image or chart, usually captioned) - This should add information to your story, usually captioned, consider adding textual commentary alongside it.
  • Narrative (foreground image, probably captioned) - This tells a story either in video or still image, add a caption for this much as you would write a script for narrative in a documentary. Ensure that the narrative details are legible in both text and visual. User should be able to control video - play, pause buttons etc.
  • Atmospheric (background video or image) - This should not interfere with legibility of the text, and should hint at narrative, foreshadow etc. Consider reducing contrast, or upping brightness to improve legibility. Video does not need controls.

The readme.md file provides a more detailed guide to the different templates (currently somewhat under construction).
